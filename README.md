# Breast-cancer-gene-exprission-python
The study focuses on ***Breast Cancer***, utilizing transcriptomic data to investigate molecular differences in gene expression between cancerous and healthy breast tissue.

 ***Gene expression data*** measures the activity level of genes, indicating how much **mRNA** is produced by a cell under specific biological conditions.

 Breast cancer tissue is fundamentally different from normal tissue due to genetic dysregulation; while

  ***normal breast tissue*** maintains stable and balanced gene expression to ensure healthy cellular function,

  ***cancer tissue*** exhibits uncontrolled growth, where oncogenes are overexpressed, and tumor suppressor genes are suppressed, and produces very high levels of genes that prevent apoptosis (such as CDK1 and ASPM). In addition, it loses its ability to express genes that stop cell division(such as p53 and BRCA1/BRCA2).


  
  The study utilized ***Microarray technology*** (specifically the Affymetrix platform) to quantify gene expression profiles  of **104** breast cancer and **17** normal breast biopsies.

  This comparison between cancer and normal tissue allows researchers to Identify Differentially Expressed Gene(DEGs), which act as biomarkers for early detection and potential therapeutic targets.

  These data provide researchers with information on which genes are uniquely activated in Cancer cells, and if we can identify a gene signature that distinguishes between benign and malignant tissues, and how
 the gene expression profile changes as breast cancer progresses.
# Exploring Gene Expression Differences Between Breast Cancer and Normal Breast Tissue
## Project Overview
This beginner bioinformatics project explores selected gene expression differences between breast cancer and normal breast tissue using Python.
## Dataset
The project is based on the public GEO dataset GSE42568.
## Aim
The aim is to practice basic biological data handling, simple comparison, visualization, and biological interpretation.
